The Pursuit of lost memories

Prologue

“We all try to live a normal life, surrounding ourselves with the people we love and the things that bring us comfort. We chase after happiness—earning money, saving what we can, spending on what brings us or those we cherish a bit of joy. In one way or another, we work to become strong, telling ourselves it’s to protect what matters most.”

“But strength is a strange thing. For some, it’s measured in wealth, for others, in knowledge or physical ability. Some excel at creative pursuits, others at sports or simple everyday skills. A few really do seem unbreakable in mind. And there are those whose strength lies in words—inspiring, persuading, drawing others in, sometimes just by being present.”

“But what about moments when none of your strengths matter? What about the nightmare you cannot wake from—the one where your loved ones are suffering and you are utterly powerless? Even the bravest soul or the brightest mind can be brought to their knees by pain that refuses to let go. How long could you stare into loss, into horror, without flinching—without wishing to turn away, to close your eyes and erase what you’ve seen? Sometimes, even just a blink gives a tiny, stolen moment of relief. But what if blinking is the only escape you have—a brief darkness before the torment floods back in as soon as you open your eyes again? Sometimes, the pain is so deep, you want nothing more than to shut your eyes forever.”

There it is—“A high-pitched, insistent WAIL!”

“What is that sound? Wouldn’t it be easier to never wake up, to stay in this darkness, away from memories that haunt and ache? Until we face such impossible moments, can any of us truly claim to know our own strength? What would you actually do if all comfort slipped away? How long would it take to gather whatever remains of your courage and force your eyes open, when every part of you wants to keep them closed?”

“Whether you’re alone or surrounded by hands that want to help, in the end, facing those unbearable sights and living on with your scars is a choice only you can make. No one would judge you for wanting instead to let go—letting the memories fade, keeping your eyes closed, forever.”

“Once, I thought I was strong. I believed I could carry any burden, bear any memory no matter how sharp its edges. I was wrong. Real strength isn’t found in never faltering, but in opening your eyes—again and again—no matter what waits in the darkness behind them.”

Beep-beep-beep-beep…

It’s that sound again. Annoying, relentless. But this time, I know what it is. The alarm. I don’t remember who set it or why it insists on dragging me out of the darkness, calling me back to a world I’d rather not see. But maybe… maybe it’s my time. Time to open my eyes. Finally,
